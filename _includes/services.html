Software

My software development style comes heavily from my time at Polar in 2013. During my time at Polar, I learnt about correct practices in programming. The ROS tutorials on the ROS Wiki was great but it was a little dull when I was first starting out with ROS. After finishing the ROS tutorials, I found really awesome set of tutorials on Clearpath Robotics’s support website. So for anyone that wants to play around with ROS, I highly recommend ROS tutorials from Clearpath Robotics (Thanks Martin Cote!) and to use Arduino with ROS, the rosserial Arduino tutorials on the ROS wiki are amazing (Few typos and errors corrected in my GitHub for rosserial)! I have an awesome Robotic Arm Project (RAP) that I am working with a friend of mine (Swapnil Patel) that will use ROS in a very exciting way!

Electrical

My electrical engineering background comes from varies projects, work experiences and university courses. I love to play around with Arduino and Raspberry Pi to create fun little projects using varies sensors and actuators! I am also quite interested in motor controllers and how they work. One of my small projects was to create an H-Bridge using MOSFETs and Arduino to control a powerful motor. We used PT100s and simple relay valves to control and monitor the temperature for three units. I was in charge of the whole project from start to finish, so I learnt decent amount of electrical circuitry with this project! I find electrical engineering fascinating and powerful and I can’t wait to learn more through projects, work experience and university courses!
